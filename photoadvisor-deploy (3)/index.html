    Tips

      Tick the lenses you own. The advisor uses them in recommendations on the Live and Manual tabs.

      The exposure triangle

      Aperture
      Controls light and depth. Low f/1.8 = blur. High f/16 = sharp.
      Shutter
      Controls motion. Fast freezes. Slow blurs or needs tripod.
      ISO
      Sensor sensitivity. Low = clean. High = grainy but bright.

      Canon M50 quick guide

      • Mode dial to M for full manual control
      • Main dial (top right) = shutter speed. Hold Av + turn = aperture
      • Press Q for Quick Menu — ISO, white balance, picture style
      • Enable the histogram on screen — keep it off both edges
      • M50 is clean at ISO 800–1600. Grain appears above ISO 3200
      • Dual Pixel CMOS AF is excellent for video and subject tracking

      Your lenses

      EF-M 15-45mm f/3.5-6.3 IS STM
      Kit lens. Best for landscape, travel, street. IS helps hand-holding. Use f/8 in daylight for sharpest results corner to corner.
      EF-M 22mm f/2 STM
      Your fastest lens. Best for low light, street, portrait. At f/2 it gathers 4× more light than the kit lens at f/4. Equivalent to 35mm full frame on the M50.

      Common scenarios

      Sunny day (Sunny 16 rule)
      f/16 · 1/100s · ISO 100 — classic starting point in direct sun
      Portrait
      f/2 (22mm) · 1/200s+ · ISO as low as possible · Blurred background, frozen faces
      Action / sport
      1/500–1/2000s · Open aperture wide · Raise ISO to compensate
      Night / low light
      f/2 (22mm) · 1/30–1/60s · ISO 1600–3200 max · Rest camera on a surface below 1/30s
      Landscape
      f/8–f/11 · 1/125s+ · ISO 100 · Use 15-45mm at widest for sweeping views

      Hand-hold guide (M50)

      Minimum safe shutter = 1 ÷ (focal length × 1.6 crop factor)
      • 15mm → min 1/25s · 22mm → min 1/35s · 45mm → min 1/75s
      • IS on the 15-45mm lens buys you ~3 extra stops — usable down to ~1/6s
      • 22mm has no IS — tuck elbows in, hold breath, shoot burst
